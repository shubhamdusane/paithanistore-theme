## What Determines the Price?

### 1. Silk Type — Biggest Factor

**Pure mulberry silk** is the costliest and most prestigious. The silk threads are incredibly fine and strong. A pure silk Paithani weighs between 600g and 900g.

**Semi-silk** (also called art silk or katan silk) uses artificial fibre for the body weft. It looks similar but feels different. Costs 60–70% less than pure silk.

**Cotton Paithani** uses cotton for the body and real silk for the border only. Comfortable for daily wear, starts at Rs.4,000–6,000.

### 2. Weave Time

A simple Paithani takes 15–20 days to weave on a handloom.
A complex Bangdi Mor or Petkar design can take 3–6 months.
The weaver earns Rs.400–800 per day. This labour cost is directly reflected in the price.

### 3. Zari Quality

**Asli (real) zari** uses actual gold or silver thread wrapped around a silk core. Very expensive — adds Rs.5,000–20,000 to the price depending on weight.

**Naqli (imitation) zari** uses copper or metallic polyester thread. Looks similar but tarnishes over time. Far cheaper.

Pure silk Paithani sarees from Yeola use real zari for the border and pallav.

### 4. Design Complexity

A plain body with a single border costs far less than a saree with an all-over brocade (filling pattern). Adding peacock, lotus, or parrot motifs throughout the body multiplies the weave time — and the price.

### 5. Weaver / Source

Buying directly from Yeola weavers (like us) removes middlemen. The same saree sold in a Mumbai or Pune showroom typically costs 40–60% more because of shop rent and distributor margins.

## Red Flags — How to Avoid Getting Cheated

❌ **Price too low:** A "pure silk GI certified Paithani" for Rs.3,000 is not possible. Silk alone costs more than that.

❌ **No GI certificate:** Genuine Yeola pure silk Paithani comes with a GI tag on the saree itself. Ask to see it.

❌ **Vague answers about silk:** A genuine seller will tell you the silk grade, the weaver's name, and how many days it took to make.

❌ **No video or real photos:** Insist on WhatsApp video calls to see the saree in natural light before buying.

❌ **No return policy:** Any trusted seller offers a return or exchange policy.
